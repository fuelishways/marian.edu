Allen Whitehill Clowes Amphitheater

This outdoor amphitheater is located on the southwest end of Marian University’s central campus green adjacent to the Mother Theresa Hackelmeier Memorial Library and Drew Hall.

Activities held in the semi-circular amphitheater enable students, faculty, staff, and visitors to enjoy both fresh air and the natural beauty of Marian’s campus.

Constructed from red brick and concrete in 1999 thanks to a gift from the Whitehill Clowes Family Foundation, the amphitheater has space to seat 400. Facing the raised center stage, notice the peaked central backdrop flanked on either side by a curved, open-air wall that defines the back of the stage. This wall features etched medallions showcasing academic programs for which Marian is known, including the humanities, sciences, and professional studies.

The stage is frequently used as a setting for back-to-school activities, pep rallies, concerts, outdoor class activities, and other events. To the front and back of the stage are spacious concrete patios with tables suitable for al fresco study sessions.

A separate structure that can be used for ticketing, concessions, equipment, and storage is also part of the facility.
