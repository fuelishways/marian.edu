Wheeler-Stokely Mansion

Wheeler-Stokely Mansion

Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, Wheeler-Stokely Mansion houses Marian University’s Office of Undergraduate Admission and Office of International Admission. Built for one of the Indianapolis Motor Speedway’s founding partners, the mansion is a Mediterranean-style villa with buff-colored brick and a green Spanish tile roof. The main entrance is through the mansion’s front door, tucked under a covered porch directly facing Cold Spring Road. A reception area is directly inside the foyer, with conference rooms and meeting spaces on either side of the lobby.

Up a grand staircase are admission counselor offices. Note the original carved wood, large limestone fireplaces, marble floors, Tiffany-style glass ceiling, light fixtures, and built-in cabinetry throughout. Outside to the west, a 320-foot covered colonnade known as the “dog walk” where the original owners walked their dog during cold and rainy weather. When Marian University purchased the estate in 1963, the grounds included a heated seven-car garage, four-story water tower, man-made lake, caretaker’s cottage, and a Japanese teahouse. Today, the caretaker’s cottage and teahouse remain. Note Art Deco tile details on what remains of the original gate, fence, and light posts.
