Doyle Hall

If you are a first-year, student who lives on Marian University’s campus, you will likely be assigned a room in Doyle Hall. Freshmen who live in Doyle form a close, tight-knit community, so plan to make friendships that last a lifetime! This L-shaped residence hall features double- and single-occupancy rooms with shared bathrooms on three floors. The average size of double rooms is 16.1 x 11.7 feet while single rooms average 8.5 x 15.10 feet. Rooms, floors, and wings are assigned by gender. All rooms and common areas are connected to Marian’s WiFi network. Rooms also wired for cable TV. Free laundry facilities and a pool table are located on the basement level. Accessible by two main entrances (one near the outdoor stadium and one facing the campus center), Doyle’s central lobby includes two large-screen LCD TVs, comfortable seating, study tables, and vending machines.

The university’s Peace and Justice Community—a dedicated living and learning space for students interested in peace and social justice issues and/or those who minor in peace and justice studies—is located in Doyle Hall. Ten resident assistants (or RAs) live in Doyle. These RAs are specially-trained peer advisors and mentors who help ensure residents have a great social and academic experience. A full-time resident director oversees Doyle’s management and daily operations. Quiet hours begin at 10 p.m. through the week and midnight on weekends; visitation ends at 12 a.m. through the week and 2 a.m. on weekends. Residents can use ID cards to enter/exit the building around-the-clock. Adjacent to St. Vincent Health Field with its 3,000-seat outdoor stadium and eight tennis courts on the southwest side of campus, the most direct route to Doyle Hall is from Tyrone Drive off of West 30th Street.
