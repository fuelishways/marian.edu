Mother Theresa Hackelmeier Memorial Library

With entrances on the north and west sides of the building, the Mother Theresa Hackelmeier Memorial Library houses about 100,000 print volumes. With digital subscriptions and databases providing access to hundreds of repositories for scholarly and research publications, the library is a hub of intellectual inquiry. The library’s circulation desk, conference rooms and collaboration suites, rotating exhibits and displays, quiet spaces for study, fit desks, a computer lab, and The Exchange, Marian’s career services and development office, are all located on the first floor.

On the second floor, you’ll find collaborative learning spaces for group study sessions and projects, quiet reading and study areas, special collections, DVDs, a reference section, and open-stack units holding thousands of books. The library’s circulating stacks are on the third floor and this entire level is a quiet study area. On the lower level, you’ll find an auditorium, offices, a 3D printing lab, the university archives, and a computer lab/classroom area.
