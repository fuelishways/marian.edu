Blessed Mother Mary Shrine and Rosary Walk

Blessed Mother Mary Shrine and Rosary Walk

Designed by architect Michael Eagan, the Blessed Mother Mary Shrine and Rosary Walk are located on the south side of Marian University's campus. Nestled into a quiet grotto-like setting near the football stadium, the shrine is adjacent to the Caretaker's Cottage and Doyle Hall.

A contemplative spot from which to pray and reflect, the open-air wood, stone, and stucco structure draws inspiration from the nearby Oriental Garden and Tea House. It shelters a statue of the Blessed Virgin Mary, which stands in front of a curved stone wall. Candles in holders are attached to this wall for those who want to light candles in prayer. Notice the tau crosses on the roof and in the brick walkway connected to the Rosary Walk.

The Rosary Walk invites you to contemplate the four Mysteries of the Rosary: the Joyful Mysteries, Sorrowful Mysteries, Glorious Mysteries, and Luminous Mysteries.

The walkway features embedded brick "stations" with polished black marble inlays represent each of the mysteries to be contemplated. Between each station, notice ten small round lights embedded in the sidewalk at regular intervals. Representing rosary beads, these lights invite the faithful to pray ten Hail Marys and a Glory Be before the next station. This pattern repeats itself until the entire rosary is prayed.
