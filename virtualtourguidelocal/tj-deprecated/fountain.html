Franciscan Heritage Fountain

Located in the center of the campus green near Alumni Hall, the Franciscan Heritage Fountain is a key Marian University landmark that was dedicated in 1998 as part of the DeHaan Family Forum.

The university's four Franciscan values—dignity of the individual, peace and justice, reconciliation, and responsible stewardship—are etched into the granite top of the round reflecting pool wall, which also serves as bench seating near the water. The Prayer of St. Francis is carved into the pedestal base of the fountain's bowl.

The fountain is surrounded by granite benches, a round brick walkway (containing some bricks inscribed with the names of Marian alumni and friends), and concrete sidewalks which radiate from the fountain like spokes on a wheel. Poles bearing the colorful flags of countries from around the world circle the fountain, signaling Marian University's commitment to global studies and welcoming international students.

The fountain is a popular gathering spot, especially during the summer when its spray provides a fine mist to cool those who sit along its ledge (no wading, please). The Franciscan Heritage Fountain is drained seasonally to prevent freezing, cracking, and ice damage during the winter months.
