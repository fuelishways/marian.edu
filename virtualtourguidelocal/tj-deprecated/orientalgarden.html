Oriental Garden and Tea House

Allen Whitehill Clowes Oriental Garden and Tea House

Marian University's Oriental Garden and Tea House is located about 400 feet southwest of Wheeler-Stokely Mansion.

The garden and tea house are part of the original estate developed by Frank Wheeler, owner of the Wheeler-Schebler Carburetor Company and founding partner in the Indianapolis Motor Speedway.

Oriental designs were popular in the early part of the 20th century. Wheeler imported the authentic tea house from Japan. At one time, the grounds also included a four-story water tower (which contained an office, apartment, and handball court), a man-made lagoon complete with an island and gondola, a gazebo, and a tennis pavilion with courts.

Historians believe Indianapolis landscape architect A.W. Brayton designed the oriental garden surrounding the tea house, although some speculate that Jens Jenson may have also contributed, especially given his work on the neighboring Allison Manson property.

When the university bought Hawkeye, as the estate was known in Wheeler's time, the garden was so overgrown it largely hid the tea house. In 1965, a gift from Indiana philanthropist Allen Whitehill Clowes enabled the university to restore the space. The effort also included a gift from the Japanese American Society of Indiana, which provided funding for a curved cedar bridge called the Friendship Bridge.

The garden was restored, refreshed, and updated with new plantings, landscaping, and rock features in 2018. It contains several semi-private nooks and seating areas. A winding natural stone walkway was restored and expanded. A circular stone patio with round fire pit and benches for seating was added. It is a relaxed, tranquil setting for special university events like receptions, teas, and yoga classes.
